Step 01 / 06

Signal Acquisition

Data collected from 700 school-aged children (ages 5–15) during school-based screening camps in collaboration with SIUT Hospital, Karachi. A Digital Auscultation System linked to a smartphone app captured both ECG and PCG signals. Only mitral valve recordings proceed to classification — mitral involvement is reported in 60.2% of RHD cases.

ECG — Electrocardiogram
Records the heart's electrical activity — rhythm, conduction, QRS morphology
PCG — Phonocardiogram
Records heart sounds — S1, systole, S2, diastole. RHD causes abnormal murmurs in silent phases
Step 02 / 06

Signal Preprocessing

ECG and PCG are preprocessed through separate pipelines because each signal carries different types of noise. Raw physiological signals contain baseline drift, powerline interference, and environmental noise — if uncorrected, these degrade the features learned by the neural network.

PCG PREPROCESSING PIPELINE
  • Noise Reduction — removes environmental noise and stethoscope interference
  • Baseline Correction — removes slow signal drift hiding heart sound components
  • Artifact Suppression — filters movement and recording disturbances
  • Resampling — standardised to fixed sampling rate
ECG PREPROCESSING PIPELINE
  • Baseline Drift Removal — corrects low-frequency wander
  • Denoising — suppresses powerline and high-frequency noise
  • Resampling — matched to PCG sampling rate
  • Amplitude Normalisation — reduces inter-subject variation

Step 03 / 06

Separate Vanilla Autoencoders

Two separate Vanilla Autoencoders — one for ECG, one for PCG — compress each clean signal into a compact 64-dimensional bottleneck. Each autoencoder learns only what matters most in its own signal, discarding noise while preserving the cardiac patterns needed for synchronization and classification.

Step 05 / 06

Feature Selection & LOPO Cross-Validation

The combined ECG + PCG bottleneck features and demographic data undergo two-stage selection, then evaluated with Leave-One-Positive-Out cross-validation — purpose-built for extreme class imbalance.

TWO-STAGE FEATURE SELECTION
  • Stage 1 — Variance Filtering: remove near-zero variance features
  • Stage 2 — Correlation Filtering: remove redundant correlated features
  • Result: reduced to 45 dimensions — ECG + PCG + demographic variables
LOPO CROSS-VALIDATION
  • Each of 6 rounds holds out one RHD-positive child for independent testing
  • No synthetic oversampling — models learn from authentic class proportions
  • Every positive case is independently evaluated at least once

Step 06 / 06

XGBoost Classification

The 45-dimensional synchronized feature set is evaluated across multiple classifiers under LOPO. XGBoost achieved perfect sensitivity of 1·00 across all 6 rounds — not a single RHD-positive child was missed — with the highest overall diagnostic score (AUPRC 0·882).
